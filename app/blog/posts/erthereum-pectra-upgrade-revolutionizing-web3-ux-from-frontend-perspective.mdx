## Before Pectra: The <HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText> Era

<HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText>, deployed in 2023, introduced
account abstraction for smart contract accounts (SCAs), enabling features like
gas sponsorship, transaction batching, and social recovery without changing
Ethereum's core protocol. For frontend engineers, this meant designing
interfaces for smart wallets (e.g., Argent, Safe) that supported paying gas in
ERC-20 tokens or recovering accounts via guardians. However,
<HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText>
had limitations:

- Externally Owned Accounts (EOAs, accounts controlled by private keys) couldn't use these features, requiring users to adopt new SCA wallets, which complicated onboarding.
- Off-chain bundlers and paymasters increased transaction costs and integration complexity.
- Users faced multi-step transaction flows, like separate approvals for token swaps, disrupting immersive dApp experiences.

> ERC-4337 empowered smart contract wallets but left EOAs behind, creating a fragmented UX that challenged frontend engineers to bridge the gap.

## Pectra's Breakthrough: <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText>

<HighlightedText>Pectra</HighlightedText>'s
<HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> integrates account abstraction into
Ethereum's core, allowing EOAs to temporarily act like SCAs by embedding smart
contract code in transactions. Unlike
<HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText>'s application-layer approach,
<HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> supports existing wallets like
MetaMask, offering:

- **Transaction Batching**: Combine actions (e.g., approve and swap) into one signature.
- **Gas Sponsorship**: Enable gasless transactions or payments in non-ETH tokens.
- **Enhanced Security**: Support delegation and social recovery without new contracts.

For frontend engineers, <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> unifies EOA and SCA capabilities, simplifying UX design. I can now create seamless flows, like one-click swaps or gasless onboarding, without managing separate account types, enhancing user engagement across dApps.

> EIP-7702 transforms EOAs into programmable wallets, streamlining UX and enabling Web2-like experiences in Web3.

## Comparing <HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText> and <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText>

To understand <HighlightedText>Pectra</HighlightedText>'s impact, here's how <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> advances beyond <HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText>:

- **Account Compatibility**: <HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText> was limited to SCAs, excluding EOAs and forcing users to adopt new wallets. <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> extends features to existing EOAs, ensuring a unified UX.
- **Protocol Integration**: <HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText> relied on off-chain infrastructure, increasing costs and complexity. <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> embeds abstraction in Ethereum's core, reducing transaction costs and simplifying adoption.
- **User Experience**: <HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText> required multi-step flows, breaking immersion. <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> enables single-signature batching, delivering smoother interactions.

<HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> builds on
<HighlightedText>ERC-4337</HighlightedText> by making account abstraction
accessible to all Ethereum accounts, lowering barriers for users and developers.

## Impact on Frontend Engineering

<HighlightedText>Pectra</HighlightedText> will elevate frontend engineering by
shifting my focus from basic blockchain connectivity to designing holistic user
journeys that harness
<HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText>’s smart account features. We will
blend intuitive interfaces that mask blockchain complexity with sophisticated
transaction flows, enabling seamless interactions like gasless transactions or
batched approvals. For users, this will translate to lower fees, faster
confirmations, and smoother dApp experiences, driving broader adoption across
DeFi, NFTs, and gaming.

## Adoption Challenges

Adopting <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> will likely introduce complexities, particularly for developers unfamiliar with smart-contract-like wallet functionalities, requiring deep study of its transaction structure through test networks. The upgrade’s novelty may bring technical risks, such as potential bugs or vulnerabilities, though rigorous community testing on testnets should mitigate some concerns. <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText>’s compatibility with existing wallet systems will ease the transition, but developers and users should remain cautious during its early stages.

> The shift to <HighlightedText>EIP-7702</HighlightedText> will demand navigating new complexities and technical risks, but its wallet compatibility will make it a transformative step for Web3 UX.
